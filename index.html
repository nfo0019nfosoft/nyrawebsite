Welcome to the Nyra hospital: where compassion meets excellence in eye care. Eye

ADVANCED CATARACT SURGERIES

Advanced cataract surgery refers to modern techniques used to remove cataracts safely.

These include laser-assisted surgery, premium lenses and customized planning which give faster recovery and clearer vision.

CORNEA

Cornea is the clear front surface of the eye that helps focus light.

Treatments include corneal transplant, laser reshaping and medical therapy for injuries and infections.

PTERYGIUM

Pterygium is a growth that develops on the white part of the eye.

Surgery removes the growth and improves comfort and vision.

ADVANCED VISION TESTS

These tests evaluate how well the eyes and brain work together.

They include retinal scans, computerized tests and visual field analysis.

RETINAL DISORDERS

Retinal disorders affect the back of the eye and can cause vision loss.

Laser therapy, injections and surgery are used to treat retinal problems.

ND-YAG LASER

Nd-YAG laser is a non-invasive eye treatment.

It is commonly used after cataract surgery to restore clear vision.

GLAUCOMA TREATMENT

Glaucoma damages the optic nerve due to high eye pressure.

Eye drops, laser and surgery help control the condition.

HUMPHREY FIELD ANALYSER

This test measures side and central vision.

It helps detect glaucoma and nerve damage early.

SQUINT SURGERY

Squint occurs when both eyes do not align properly.

Surgery corrects eye position and improves vision.

OCULOPLASTY

Oculoplasty treats eyelids, tear ducts and eye sockets.

It includes cosmetic and reconstructive eye procedures.

COMPUTERISED EYE TESTING

Digital tests are used to evaluate eye health.

They detect problems early and help plan accurate treatment.

CONTACT LENS CENTER

Provides expert fitting and care for contact lenses.

Includes soft lenses, colored lenses and special lenses.

General & Laparoscopy Surgery

HEMORRHOIDS

Hemorrhoids, also known as piles, are swollen veins in the lower rectum and anus that can cause pain, itching and bleeding during bowel movements.

They can be internal or external and are commonly caused by constipation, pregnancy or prolonged sitting. Advanced laser and minimally invasive surgical treatments provide fast relief and long-term cure with minimal discomfort.

FISSURES

Anal fissures are small painful tears in the lining of the anus that usually occur due to passing hard or large stools.

They cause severe pain and bleeding during bowel movements. Treatment includes medicines, diet changes, and in severe cases, minimally invasive surgery that provides long-term healing.

FISTULA IN ANO

Fistula is an abnormal tunnel that forms between the anal canal and the skin near the anus, often leading to discharge and infection.

It usually develops after an abscess and requires surgical treatment. Laser and minimally invasive procedures allow precise closure with quicker recovery and less pain.

HERNIA

A hernia occurs when an organ or tissue pushes through a weak spot in the surrounding muscle wall, forming a visible bulge.

Laparoscopic hernia repair is a modern technique that uses small incisions to strengthen the weakened area, allowing faster healing and reduced recurrence.

HYDROCELE

Hydrocele is a painless swelling of the scrotum caused by the accumulation of fluid around a testicle.

It can cause discomfort and heaviness. A simple surgical procedure permanently removes the fluid and restores normal appearance.

APPENDIX

Appendicitis is inflammation of the appendix and is one of the most common surgical emergencies.

Laparoscopic appendectomy is the preferred treatment, allowing the infected appendix to be removed safely with minimal scarring and quick recovery.

GALLSTONES

Gallstones are hard deposits that form in the gallbladder and can cause severe abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting.

Laparoscopic gallbladder removal is a safe and effective solution that prevents future stone formation and complications.

VARICOSE VEINS

Varicose veins are enlarged, twisted veins that usually appear in the legs and can cause pain and swelling.

Modern laser and minimally invasive procedures improve blood flow, relieve symptoms and enhance cosmetic appearance.

CIRCUMCISION

Circumcision is a surgical procedure that removes the foreskin from the penis for medical or hygienic reasons.

It is commonly performed to treat infections, tight foreskin and improve hygiene. Modern techniques ensure minimal pain and fast healing.

HYSTERECTOMY

Hysterectomy is a surgical procedure to remove the uterus in women suffering from fibroids, bleeding or cancer.

Laparoscopic hysterectomy offers smaller incisions, reduced pain and quicker return to normal activities.

TUBECTOMY

Tubectomy is a permanent family planning method in which the fallopian tubes are blocked or cut.

It prevents pregnancy permanently and is done through a simple minimally invasive surgical procedure.

THYROIDECTOMY

Thyroidectomy is the surgical removal of part or all of the thyroid gland.

It is performed to treat thyroid cancer, nodules or hormone disorders and is done with high precision for safe recovery.

Pediatric Surgery

ANTENATAL

Antenatal scans can detect birth defects before the baby is born, allowing doctors to plan treatment early.

Some abnormalities can be corrected during pregnancy or immediately after birth, improving survival and quality of life for the child.

MINIMAL INVASIVE

Minimal invasive surgery, also called keyhole surgery, uses tiny instruments and cameras to perform operations.

It reduces pain, scarring and hospital stay while allowing faster recovery for children.

NEONATAL SURGERY

Neonatal surgery is performed on newborns less than one month old for life-threatening conditions.

These surgeries are often done in emergencies to correct birth defects and save the baby’s life.

PEDIATRIC CONTINENCE

Continence refers to control of bladder and bowel movements in children.

Specialized treatment programs help children with bedwetting, constipation and bowel control issues.

THORACIC SURGERY

Thoracic surgery treats diseases of the chest including lungs and food pipe in children.

It is used for infections, tumors and congenital chest abnormalities.

UROLOGY

Pediatric urology treats kidney, bladder and urinary problems in children.

Conditions like urine infection, blockage and birth defects are treated surgically when required.
